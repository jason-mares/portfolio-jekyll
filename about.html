FAQ

How does your design process work?

We schedule an initial meeting to figure out what your situation is. From there we can get more in depth about how you want to come across to your audience, who your users are and ways to increase revenue, business efficiency and awareness. The more complex the problem is, the more we collaborate. When work begins, your approval will be required at different milestones to move the project forward.

I just need something quick, can we skip the research?

Designers solve problems for their clients. We cannot solve a problem together if we don't understand it. Research and collaboration are crucial for your success. An auto mechanic would never start fixing your car without first performing a diagnostic that not only factors in your description of the problem, but how that problem affects the entire car. Your business or ideas are much more valuable than a car.

What if I already have an idea of what I want?

Samples and inspiration are welcome but ultimately your logo, website, poster, etc has to work for the people you're trying to communicate with. Most clients know their users well enough not to request pink unicorns in a website designed to be used by Harley riders, but it's my professional responsibility to make sure I'm not just taking your money without question. How much would you trust a doctor that gave out medication without discussing with patients why they were asking for it?

I need a (website, logo, poster, etc) how much do you charge?

It depends on your budget, deadline and value/risk ratio. Shoot me an email for an estimate.

Can I edit your designs?

If you have paid for your project in full I will transfer the copyright over to you. If you own all the right software to edit the files (I do not provide free copies of software) then it's yours to do what you want.

You seem really skilled, are you one of those "jack of all trades master of none?"

No, I just love what I do. A supportive English teacher taught me how to code when I was 13 and I got better by creating custom myspace profiles for my friends. I got my first paying freelance gig shortly after graduating high school then never thought about doing anything else. This discipline captures my fascination and I'm constantly learning new ways to improve.
